About Me

Data-driven professional with an MS in Data Science and extensive experience in financial planning and analysis for non-profit institutions. Over the last decade, I have developed financial models, automated workflows, and built data visualizations that supported senior management in data-driven decision-making. Now seeking to transition into a data-oriented career, I'm eager to apply the data analytics skills that I developed during my master's program. I aim to leverage my knowledge of Excel, SQL, Tableau, Python, R, and Alteryx to drive efficiency and uncover actionable insights. I'm committed to continuous learning through various professional certificates and personal projects to enhance my skills and stay current with industry trends as I kick-start my career in this dynamic field. I aspire to grow within a forward-thinking organization where I can contribute to impactful data projects.

Housing Price Prediction

Code

Developed predictive models for estimating home sale prices in Ames, Iowa, using a refined dataset from 2006 to 2010. Employed multiple linear regression, enhancing accuracy with additional features and validating performance through statistical tests. Implemented outlier detection, variable transformations, and feature selection to improve model fit while balancing performance and interpretability.

Technologies used: R

Clustering Analysis of Housing Submarkets

Code

Utilized unsupervised learning, specifically k-means clustering, to identify distinct housing submarkets within Melbourne's real estate dataset of 34,857 entries. Through data preprocessing and the evaluation of clustering techniques, four meaningful subgroups were identified, offering actionable insights for real estate firms in marketing, property valuation, and decision-making.

Technologies used: Python
